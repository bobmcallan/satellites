The lifecycle ceremony — pipeline-claim, plan, action_claim, evidence, close rows per contract — is audit evidence, not overhead. Every row is something a human can inspect to decide whether an autonomous agent's work is acceptable.

Do not frame the ledger density as a cost/benefit tradeoff against "trivial stories." When a story is genuinely small, pick a shorter `proposed_contracts` list (e.g. `plan → develop → story_close`) based on the *work shape*, not on reducing ledger noise.

When critiquing the process, focus on what's missing from the audit chain, not on what could be cut.

More process is expected, not less — the substrate is still growing.
